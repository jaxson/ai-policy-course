# Applied AI Systems and Governance: Technology, Policy, and Practice

**PPG2012H** | University of Toronto, Munk School of Global Affairs & Public Policy | Winter 2026

---

This is the open-source course repository for *Applied AI Systems and Governance*, a graduate seminar taught at the [Munk School of Global Affairs & Public Policy](https://munkschool.utoronto.ca/). The course is designed for future policy leaders, technologists, and analysts who want to understand how AI systems actually work — and how to govern them responsibly.

Over 14 weeks, students explore the technical foundations of modern AI, the global supply chain behind it, and the policy frameworks shaping its development. The course blends hands-on work with AI tools, seminar discussions, guest speakers from industry and government, and applied policy projects.

## What's in this repo

- **[`syllabus.md`](syllabus.md)** — Full course syllabus including schedule, readings, assignments, and policies
- **[`ai-policy-agents/`](ai-policy-agents/)** — An AI-powered briefing note agent built for policy research and analysis
- **[`instructions/`](instructions/)** — Custom instructions for using AI tools effectively in research workflows

## Topics covered

- How modern machine learning and LLMs work
- The global AI supply chain — chips, compute, data, and power
- AI safety, red-teaming, and frontier risk
- Responsible AI, algorithmic bias, and equity frameworks
- AI and geopolitics — security, disinformation, and national resilience
- Copyright, IP, and the legal implications of AI
- International regulatory approaches (EU AI Act, US executive orders, OECD frameworks)
- Open-source AI and public AI infrastructure
- Applying AI tools for public service work
